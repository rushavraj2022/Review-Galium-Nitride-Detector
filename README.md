# Review-Galium-Nitride-Detector
Gallium Nitride (GaN) Semiconductor Detectors for Natural Radiation &amp; Extreme Environments


--------------------------------------Abstract--------------------------------------

The demand for stable radiation detection in extreme and natural environments has driven a critical transition from traditional silicon and germanium semiconductors to wide band-gap materials. This review comprehensively examines the implementation of Gallium Nitride (GaN) as a premier semiconductor matrix for radiation dosimetry and spectroscopy. With a wide direct band-gap of 3.39 eV, a remarkable high defect displacement threshold and superior thermal and chemical stability, GaN bypasses cryogenic cooling requirements inherent to legacy materials. The review traces the shift from highly defective heteroepitaxial growth to GaN-on-GaN homoepitaxy, evaluates empirical advancements in alpha particle spectroscopy, intrinsic and converter-assisted thermal neutron detection, and X-ray sensitivity amplification using AlGaN/GaN 2DEG architectures. Emerging integration of AI/ML in crystal growth acceleration, radiation damage modelling, and particle discrimination is highlighted. GaN's resilience at >500 °C, in intense cosmological fields, and in caustic radioactive liquids is assessed, alongside utility in monitoring NORM and radon gas.
